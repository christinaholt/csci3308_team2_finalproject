How to Choose an Appropriate Weight for Resistance Exercise

______________________________________________________________

Safety is most important! Always check with your healthcare provider before starting an exercise regime.


When participating in resistance training exercises, it is important to ensure that you are lifting an appropriate weight.

Consider the size of the muscle group(s) that you will be working for each particular exercise:

    For larger muscle groups (ex. glutes, quads, back, chest, hamstrings) = select a heavier starting weight

    For smaller muscle groups (ex. shoulders, biceps, triceps, calves) = select a lighter starting weight


Good form reduces injury. Always select a weight that you are able to maintain good form with for the entirety of the exercise.

Go slow, momentum is cheating.

______________________________________________________________


As a general starting point, for each exercise find the heaviest weight that you can complete 1x with good form. This is your One Rep Max (1RM)


To determine the amount of weight to use for the exercise, reduce your 1RM by the following percentage based on your workout goal:

    Build Muscle Mass = Reduce 1RM by 50%

    Muscular Strength = Reduce 1RM by 60%

    Muscular Endurance = Reduce 1RM by 70%


If you find that you are able to complete 1-2 more reps than the recommended number in each set, increase weight by 2-10% until you reach fatigue within the set.
